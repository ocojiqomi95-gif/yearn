🚀 Expedition

About
Expedition

The story of how Yearn Finance became one of DeFi's most respected and enduring yield optimization protocols — from a single developer's experiment to a fully decentralized community-owned protocol.

🌱 Origins

How Yearn
Was Born

Yearn Finance began as iEarn — a passion project created by Andre Cronje in early 2020. Andre, a DeFi developer frustrated by the manual effort required to chase the best yields across lending protocols, built a simple automated rebalancer that moved funds between Compound, Aave, and dYdX.

The protocol quickly attracted users who valued automation over manual yield chasing. As the product grew more sophisticated, the need for governance and community ownership became clear. In July 2020, the YFI governance token was launched — famously distributed with zero team allocation, zero founder pre-mine, and no investor allocation. YFI was distributed entirely to users of the protocol.

This radical fairness ethos created one of DeFi's most passionate and engaged communities. Within days, YFI became one of the most valuable tokens in crypto, cementing Yearn's place in DeFi history.

🏗️

Built by the Community

Yearn is governed and maintained by an entirely volunteer-driven community. There are no employees, no corporate structure, and no centralized control. Everything is coordinated through on-chain governance and community collaboration.

🎯 Mission

Our Mission &
Values

🌍

Open Finance for All

Yearn believes that high-quality yield strategies should be accessible to everyone — not just institutional investors. Our protocol is permissionless, non-custodial, and available to anyone with an internet connection and an Ethereum wallet.

🔓

Radical Transparency

Every aspect of Yearn's operations is publicly verifiable on-chain. Strategy code is open source, vault allocations are visible to all, governance votes are on-chain, and financial flows are fully transparent. We believe transparency builds trust.

🤝

Community Ownership

Yearn is owned and governed by its YFI token holders. Protocol parameters, treasury management, and strategic direction are all determined through community proposals and on-chain votes. No single entity controls Yearn's future.

📅 History

Yearn's
Journey

2020

January 2020 — iEarn Launch

Andre Cronje launches iEarn, an automated yield optimizer that moves funds between Compound, Aave, and dYdX to find the best lending rates.

Jul

July 2020 — YFI Launch & Fair Distribution

YFI governance token launches with a revolutionary fair distribution model — zero allocation to founders, investors, or team. 30,000 YFI distributed entirely to protocol users, creating DeFi history and sparking a massive community.

Sep

September 2020 — Vault V1

Yearn Vaults V1 launch, introducing the concept of multi-strategy yield optimization. Users could deposit ETH, USDC, DAI, and other assets to earn automated DeFi yields.

2021

2021 — Vault V2 & yCRV

Vault V2 introduces multiple strategies per vault and more sophisticated allocation mechanisms. yCRV launches as Yearn's first liquid locker product, leveraging Yearn's growing veCRV position.

2023

2023 — Vault V3 (ERC-4626)

Yearn launches Vault V3, fully embracing the ERC-4626 tokenized vault standard. V3 introduces a more modular architecture, enabling permissionless strategy creation and broader composability across the DeFi ecosystem.

Now

Present — Multi-Chain Expansion

Yearn continues expanding across EVM chains, building new products like stYFI, yYB, and advanced tooling. The community drives development, governance, and strategy creation with an ever-growing contributor base.

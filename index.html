❓ FAQ

Frequently Asked
Questions

Yearn Finance is a decentralized finance (DeFi) protocol that aggregates and automates yield farming strategies across multiple lending protocols and liquidity pools. Users deposit assets into Yearn Vaults, and smart contracts automatically deploy capital to the highest-yielding opportunities available on Ethereum and other EVM chains.

yVaults are smart contracts that accept user deposits and automatically deploy them across multiple yield strategies. Each vault has one or more strategies — automated scripts that interact with DeFi protocols like Aave, Compound, and Curve. Strategies harvest rewards, compound returns, and rebalance allocations to maximize APY. Users receive vault tokens (yvTokens) representing their share of the vault's assets.

YFI is Yearn's governance token. YFI holders can vote on protocol proposals, fee structures, and development priorities through Yearn's on-chain governance system. By staking YFI as stYFI, holders also earn a share of protocol fees generated by Yearn's products. YFI has a fixed supply of 36,666 tokens, making it one of the scarcer governance tokens in DeFi.

Yearn takes security extremely seriously. All smart contracts undergo multiple independent audits before deployment. The protocol uses timelocks, multisig controls, and an emergency shutdown mechanism to protect user funds. Yearn has maintained an excellent security track record since its launch in 2020. However, as with all DeFi protocols, there are inherent smart contract risks users should be aware of before depositing funds.

Yearn vaults charge a performance fee on profits and a management fee on total assets. These fees are used to compensate strategists, fund protocol development, and distribute rewards to YFI stakers. The exact fee structure varies by vault. You can always review current fee parameters on the Yearn app or in the governance documentation. There are no withdrawal or deposit fees.

yCRV is Yearn's liquid locker for veCRV (vote-escrowed CRV). Instead of locking CRV tokens for up to 4 years, users can deposit CRV to receive yCRV, which is liquid and tradeable. Yearn maintains a large veCRV position to maximize voting power on Curve gauges, boost yields for yCRV holders, and earn Curve admin fees — all of which are distributed to yCRV holders and Yearn vault depositors.
